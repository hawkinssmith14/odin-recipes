Authentic Polish Meat Jelly!

Polish meat jelly, known as galareta or zimne nóżki, is a traditional dish made from slow-cooked pork trotters, knuckles, or other gelatin-rich meats, which solidify into a flavorful, savory jelly when chilled. Often served cold with vinegar, lemon juice, or a bit of mustard, this dish is a beloved appetizer in Poland, especially around holidays and family gatherings. Its rich texture and deep umami taste make it a unique and satisfying dish for those who appreciate old-world culinary traditions.

Ingredients

For the broth:

  • 2 pork trotters (or substitute with pork knuckles or beef shank)
  • 500g pork shoulder (or chicken for a lighter version)
  • 1 carrot, peeled
  • 1 parsnip, peeled
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon marjoram (optional)
  • 2 litres water

For serving:

  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • White vinegar, lemon juice, or mustard

Steps

Prepare the meat:

  1. Clean the pork trotters thoroughly and place them in large pot.
  2. Add the pork shoulder, coarrot, parsnip, onion, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns.

Simmer the broth:

  1. Pour in enough water to fully cover the ingredients (about 2 litres)
  2. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Skim off any foam that rises to the top.
  3. Simmer uncovered for about 3-4 hours, until the meat is falling off the bones and the broth is rich.

Strain and season:

  1. Remove the meat and vegetables from the pot. Discard the bay leaves and peppercorns.
  2. Strain the broth through a fine sieve or cheesecloth to remove impurities.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and marjoram, if using.

Shred the meat:

  1. Separate the meat from the bones and cartilage, then shred it into small pieces.
  2. Optionally, dice the carrot and add it back for color.

Assemble and chill:

  1. Arrange slices of hard-boiled egg in serving bowls or molds.
  2. Distribute the shredded meat evenly, then pour the strained broth over it.
  3. Let the mixture cool slightly before placing it in the refrigerator. Chill for 6–8 hours or overnight until fully set.

Serve and enjoy:

  1. Remove from the fridge and garnish with fresh parsley.
  2. Serve with a side of vinegar, lemon juice, or mustard for extra flavor.
